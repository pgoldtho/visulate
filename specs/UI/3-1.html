Developer Notes

The receivable screen creates rows in the RNT_PAYMENTS, RNT_ACCOUNTS_RECEIVABLE and RNT_PAYMENT_ALLOCATIONS tables. The RNT_PAYMENTS table is used to record details of payments received. RNT_ACCOUNTS_RECEIVABLE records details of payments due and RNT_PAYMENT_ALLOCATIONS records details of payment allocations.

The Unallocated Payments section of the screen is used to record RNT_PAYMENTS records. It can be used as a holding area for payments that the manager is unable to assign. Sometimes a tenant will make a bank payment without providing identification. For example, they may make a $600 cash deposit without writing their name on the deposit slip (these aren't the brightest people in the world). The property manager can record the payment as an unallocated payment while they try to figure out who it came from. They can also use unallocated payments when they receive a single payment for more than one item. For example, a property manager may receive a payment of $800 from a tenant who owes $750 in rent and $100 for repairs. The manager must decide how to allocate the payment. She could decide to allocte $750 to rent and $50 for repairs or $100 for repairs and $700 for rent. The radio buttons on the screen would be used to do this. The Allocated and Balance totals should keep a running total of the amount allocated and remaining.

Not all payment require an entry in the RNT_PAYMENTS table. They can be recorded direclty in the RNT_PAYMENT_ALLOCATIONS table and joined to a row in RNT_ACCOUNTS_RECEIVABLE. RNT_ACCOUNTS_RECEIVABLE rows are created from this screen by pressing the Update List buttons for Rent Due or Recoverable Items or by inserting a new row in the Other Payments section of the screen.

Most payments are made from the Rent Due section of this screen. The user clicks on the "Update List" button to generate a list of payments due on the current date. It does this by reading the tenancy agreements recorded in the system and generating an entry for each agreement where the agreement end date is null and the current date >= last RNT_ACCOUNTS_RECEIVABLE record + RNT_TENANCY_AGREEMENT.AMOUNT_PERIOD. It should also look at the late fee entries for the agreement and generate fees for overdue payments.

The user should be able to populate the Amount Paid and Date Paid fields on the screen by pressing the Paid button on the screen. The system should copy the Amount Due value to the Amount Paid field and the current date to the Date Paid field. The user should be able to edit these values before commiting them. The user should also be able to remove rows by pressing the remove button. This should set the amount due value to $0 to prevent the system from regenerating the entry the next time the screen is populated.

Recoverable items relate to repairs or agreement actions that the property manger has marked as recoverable.
